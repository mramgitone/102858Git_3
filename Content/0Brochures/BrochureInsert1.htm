Helios DAS converts a single optical into the equivalent of tens of thousands of highly-sensitive vibrational sensors. Using a standard single mode telecommunications cable that is deployed in-well or connected to a linear asset Helios monitors vibration disturbances along optical s up to 40km. It provides dynamic real-time information to operatives in a variety of industries and can be used independently or with our proprietary alarm management server, Panoptes.

Principle of DAS

As an advanced variant of Optical Time Domain Reflectometer (OTDR) instrumentation, Helios monitors the Rayleigh backscatter noise signature that is fine structured in the backscatter signature of the optic cable. Helios is optimized to measure small changes in the coherent Rayleigh noise structure that occurs from pulse to pulse and very small physical (acoustic or vibration) disturbances at a point in the can make detectable changes in the interferometric signal.

The Helios interrogator detects simultaneous acoustic disturbances along the entire to provide real-time information that details the time, location and types of disturbance.

Features

  • Simple to install as an operation is being developed or retrospectively on existing infrastructure
  • Low deployment cost, it operates with standard telecommunication grade optical s
  • Lower “through life” maintenance costs
  • Requires no power along the entire sensor length beyond the interrogator unit. The system is immune to EM and RF interference
  • User friendly system configuration and alarm handling software that enables a high degree of tuning and simple integration with client management systems
