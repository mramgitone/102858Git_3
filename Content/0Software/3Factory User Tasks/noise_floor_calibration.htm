Calibration of the Noise Floor

In addition to the Noise Floor Bounding controls, Factory Users have the ability to “train” the system in order to create the noise floor model. There are two steps to this process:

  • Collection of the trained data, which can take time.
  • Calibration of the model.

Factory Calibration

Before it is sent to you, the system will be calibrated at the factory, in a controlled environment. If your system was purchased before this functionality was available, or it has not been activated for any reason, contact Customer Services to arrange for your system to be returned.

If you are a Fotech Engineer, refer to Factory Procedure for Noise Floor Calibration. It can be requirement to suppress alarms during this procedure. Refer to How to suppress Alarm Reports.

Calibration in the field

Any system that was purchased with version 5.0 of the software, or greater, will have the calibration performed at the factory. However systems that are already in the field can be upgraded to version without this calibration having been performed. In this case, in the Noise Floor Bounding Tab you will see the black light, mentioned above. We recommend that the system is returned to the factory for the full calibration.

However it may be that such a procedure is not viable, either due to the costs or to the available time. In those cases, a field engineer with Factory Level permissions may be able to perform enough of a calibration to provide a useful, albeit sub-optimal, result. The instructions for this are below:

  1. Disconnect the system from the deployed .
  2. Replace the system with a spool of similar length, which should be at least as long as the deployed .

Find as quiet a location as you can in which to place the spool. The more noise there is while the data is collected, the less accurate the model will be.

  1. Collect the training data. A full sweep is not done due to time constraints, but do enough to cover the portion of the model that interests us. It can take many hours or even days, and is dependant upon the maximum, minimum and step sizes involved.

Make sure the values are set for the Raw Data display before these instructions are completed. If values have not been set, the calibration will be a waste of time and the data collection exercise will have to be repeated.

To determine the portion of the model required refer to Definition of the Portion of the Model.
